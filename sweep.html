Empowering Future Female Leaders

Join the UNIVEN Student Women Economic Empowerment Programme (SWEEP) Chapter for 2025/26. We are looking for young, dynamic student women who are proactive, innovative and interested in entrepreneurship, employability and sisterhood circle.

Leadership Development

Build leadership skills through 7 executive positions in the SWEEP 2026 team

Sisterhood Circle

Join a supportive community of ambitious women for networking and collaboration

Employability Skills

Develop skills that enhance your career prospects and entrepreneurial potential

Program Highlights:

  • Part of UNIVEN's student-led entrepreneurship ecosystem
  • Under the Directorate of Community Engagement, Entrepreneurship, Inclusive Innovation and Commercialisation
  • Integration with other student programs: ESAP, EDHE, Map the System
  • Focus on sustainable development and community engagement

Eligibility Requirements

Who can apply for SWEEP Executive positions

Female Student at UNIVEN

Currently enrolled as a female student at University of Venda for the 2025 academic year

Not in Final Year

2025 must NOT be your final year of study (you should be returning in 2026)

Creative & Innovative

Demonstrate creativity, innovative thinking, and problem-solving abilities

Positive Attitude

Maintain a positive, solution-oriented mindset with resilience and determination

Team Player

Ability to work effectively in teams and collaborate with diverse individuals

Time Management

Flexible with good time management skills to balance studies and leadership role

Communication Skills

Have good verbal and written communication skills in English

Entrepreneurial Spirit

Demonstrate entrepreneurial spirit, drive, and interest in economic empowerment

SWEEP in the UCfERI Ecosystem

Part of UNIVEN's comprehensive entrepreneurship support system

Directorate Structure

SWEEP operates under the Directorate of Community Engagement, Entrepreneurship, Inclusive Innovation and Commercialisation (DCEEIIC), ensuring institutional support and resources.

Student-Led Programs Network

Connected with other student initiatives: Entrepreneurship Student Ambassadors Programme (ESAP), Indigenous Knowledge Systems Ambassadors Programme (IKSAP), Map the System, Sustainable Beekeeping, Black Management Forum-Student Chapter (BMFSC), StoptheSpot, Skills for the Future Programme (SFFP), and Enactus.

University Integration

SWEEP executives work closely with UCfERI, Economic Activation Office, Community Engagement unit, and other university departments to implement women empowerment programs.

External Partnerships

Access to partnerships with SEDFA, ABSA Bank, Vhembe District Municipality, NIPMO, TIA, and other industry partners through UCfERI networks.
